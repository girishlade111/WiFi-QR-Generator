General Questions

What is a Wi-Fi QR Code?

A Wi-Fi QR Code is a scannable matrix barcode that encodes your Wi-Fi network credentials in a standardized format. When someone scans the code with their smartphone camera or QR reader app, their device automatically recognizes the Wi-Fi information and offers to connect to the network without requiring manual password entry.

The QR code follows the Wi-Fi Alliance specification and works with iOS (iOS 11+), Android (Android 10+), and most QR scanning applications.

How does Wi-Fi QR Code Generator work?

Wi-Fi QR Code Generator creates a specially formatted string that follows the standard Wi-Fi QR code specification: 

WIFI:T:WPA;S:MyNetwork;P:MyPassword;H:false;;

This string contains:

  • T: Security type (WPA, WEP, or nopass)
  • S: Network name (SSID)
  • P: Password (for secured networks)
  • H: Hidden network status (true/false)

The generator then converts this string into a QR code matrix that can be scanned by any compatible device.

Yes, with a simple setup. By default, the QR code library is loaded from a CDN (Content Delivery Network). To use the generator completely offline:

Once loaded, the generator works without an internet connection since all processing happens in your browser.

  • iOS: iPhone and iPad with iOS 11 or later (use the built-in Camera app)
  • Android: Android 10 or later (use the built-in Camera app or Quick Settings)
  • Other devices: Any device with a QR scanning app that supports Wi-Fi credentials

For older devices, third-party QR scanner apps from the App Store or Google Play can also read Wi-Fi QR codes.

Security & Privacy

Yes, your Wi-Fi password is completely safe. Wi-Fi QR Code Generator is designed with privacy as a core principle:

  • Your password never leaves your device

Should I share my Wi-Fi QR Code publicly?

Be cautious when sharing Wi-Fi QR codes publicly. Anyone who scans the code will have access to your network credentials. Consider these best practices:

  • Use a guest network: Create a separate network for guests with limited access
  • Display in controlled areas: Only place QR codes where intended users can access them
  • Rotate passwords periodically: Change your Wi-Fi password and regenerate QR codes regularly
  • Monitor network access: Keep an eye on connected devices in your router settings

What security types are supported?

Wi-Fi QR Code Generator supports the following security types:

  • WPA/WPA2: Most common modern security (password 8-63 characters)
  • WEP: Older security standard (5 or 13 ASCII characters, or 10/26 hex)
  • No Password (Open): For networks without authentication

WPA3 is not directly supported in the QR code specification, but WPA3 networks that support WPA2 backward compatibility will work with WPA2-selected QR codes.

Can someone steal my password from the QR code?

Yes, anyone who scans your Wi-Fi QR code can obtain your network password. This is by design - the QR code is meant to share credentials conveniently.

To protect your network:

  • Only share QR codes with trusted individuals
  • Use a guest network for public or semi-public sharing
  • Regularly monitor connected devices
  • Change passwords periodically if QR codes are widely distributed

Usage & How-To

How do I create a Wi-Fi QR Code?

Creating a Wi-Fi QR Code is simple:

  1. Enter your Wi-Fi network name (SSID) in the first field
  2. Enter your Wi-Fi password
  3. Select your security type (WPA/WPA2, WEP, or No password)
  4. Check "Hidden network" if your SSID is not broadcast
  5. Adjust QR size and error correction if needed
  6. Choose export format (PNG or SVG)
  7. Click "Generate QR Code"
  8. Scan with your phone or download the image

What should I enter for the SSID?

Enter your exact Wi-Fi network name (SSID) as it appears when searching for networks. The SSID is case-sensitive, so make sure to match the capitalization exactly.

Examples:

  • "HomeNetwork" (not "homenetwork" or "HOMENETWORK")
  • "CoffeeShop_Guest" (include underscores and special characters)
  • "ATT-WiFi-5G" (include hyphens)

If you're unsure of your SSID, check your router's label, admin panel, or look at the Wi-Fi settings of a already-connected device.

What are the password requirements?

Password requirements depend on your security type:

  • WPA/WPA2: 8-63 characters (ASCII) or exactly 64 hex characters
  • WEP: 5 or 13 ASCII characters, OR 10 or 26 hexadecimal characters
  • No Password: Leave the password field empty

Special characters (!@#$%^&*etc.) are supported and should be entered exactly as they appear in your password.

What does "Hidden network" mean?

A hidden network is one that doesn't broadcast its SSID (network name). When you check "Hidden network," the QR code includes a flag that tells connecting devices to actively probe for your network rather than waiting for it to appear in the list.

Only check this option if you've specifically configured your router to hide the SSID. Most networks do not need this option enabled.

What QR size should I choose?

QR size affects the visual dimensions of the generated image:

  • Small (200-400px): Good for digital display, small prints
  • Medium (400-600px): Standard size for most uses
  • Large (600px+): Best for large prints, posters, or high-resolution needs

The scannability depends more on print quality and distance than pixel size. For physical prints, ensure the final printed QR code is at least 2x2 cm (about 1 inch).

What is error correction?

Error correction determines how much of the QR code can be damaged or obscured while still remaining scannable:

  • L (Low - 7%): Smallest QR code, least damage tolerance
  • M (Medium - 15%): Good balance, recommended for most uses
  • Q (Quartile - 25%): Higher tolerance, denser pattern
  • H (High - 30%): Maximum tolerance, densest pattern

Higher error correction makes the QR code more robust but also more visually complex (more dots). For most uses, Medium (M) is recommended.

PNG or SVG - which format should I choose?

Both formats have their advantages:

  • PNG: Raster image, universally compatible, good for digital use and standard printing
  • SVG: Vector format, infinitely scalable without quality loss, ideal for professional printing and large formats

Choose PNG for everyday use (social media, documents, small prints). Choose SVG for professional printing, large posters, or if you need to edit the QR code in design software.

How do I scan a Wi-Fi QR Code?

On iPhone/iPad (iOS 11+):

  1. Open the Camera app
  2. Point at the QR code
  3. Tap the notification that appears
  4. Confirm to join the network

On Android (Android 10+):

  1. Open the Camera app or Quick Settings Wi-Fi panel
  2. Point at the QR code
  3. Tap the notification or prompt
  4. Confirm to connect

Troubleshooting

My QR code won't scan. What's wrong?

Several factors can prevent successful scanning:

  • Poor print quality: Ensure the QR code is printed clearly without smudges
  • Too small: Make sure the printed QR code is at least 2x2 cm (1 inch)
  • Glare or reflections: Avoid glossy surfaces that create glare
  • Low lighting: Ensure adequate lighting when scanning
  • Camera focus: Hold steady and allow the camera to focus
  • Wrong distance: Try moving closer or farther from the code

Try regenerating with higher error correction (Q or H) for better scan reliability.

Phone scans the code but won't connect

If scanning works but connection fails, check:

  • Password accuracy: Verify the password in the QR code matches your current password
  • Security type: Ensure the correct security type was selected (WPA vs WEP)
  • Network availability: Confirm the network is broadcasting and within range
  • MAC filtering: Check if your router has MAC address filtering enabled
  • Device limit: Some routers limit the number of connected devices
  • Hidden network: If enabled, ensure the SSID is entered exactly

WPA password is rejected

WPA/WPA2 passwords must be:

  • 8-63 ASCII characters, OR
  • Exactly 64 hexadecimal characters (0-9, A-F)

If your password doesn't meet these requirements, you'll need to change it in your router settings. Also verify there are no extra spaces before or after the password.

WEP password is rejected

WEP passwords have specific format requirements:

  • 5 ASCII characters (any keyboard characters), OR
  • 13 ASCII characters (any keyboard characters), OR
  • 10 hexadecimal characters (0-9, A-F), OR
  • 26 hexadecimal characters (0-9, A-F)

Note: WEP is an outdated and insecure protocol. Consider upgrading your router to WPA2 or WPA3 if possible.

The generator isn't loading

If the page doesn't load properly:

  • Check your connection: Ensure you have internet access
  • Clear browser cache: Try clearing cache and cookies
  • Try a different browser: Some browsers may have compatibility issues
  • Disable extensions: Ad blockers or security extensions may interfere
  • Check JavaScript: Ensure JavaScript is enabled in your browser

Download doesn't work

If downloading the QR code fails:

  • Generate first: Make sure you've clicked "Generate QR Code"
  • Check browser settings: Some browsers block automatic downloads
  • Try right-click: Right-click the QR code and select "Save Image As"
  • Try screenshot: Take a screenshot of the QR code as an alternative
  • Switch format: Try the other format (PNG vs SVG)
