Privacy Policy

Your Privacy Matters

Transparent, privacy-focused design. Your data never leaves your device.

Last Updated: March 12, 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to Wi-Fi QR Code Generator ("we," "us," "our," or "the Service"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring that your use of our service is safe and secure. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our website and services.

Our Core Privacy Principle: Wi-Fi QR Code Generator operates on a fundamental principle - your data is yours. We have designed our service to collect, store, and transmit as little information as possible. In fact, we collect nothing.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information We DO NOT Collect

Wi-Fi QR Code Generator is designed with privacy at its core. We want to be completely transparent about what information we do not collect:

  • No Wi-Fi Credentials: Your SSID (network name) and password never leave your device. They are processed locally in your browser and are never transmitted to any server.
  • No Personal Information: We do not collect names, email addresses, phone numbers, or any other personally identifiable information.
  • No Usage Data: We do not track which features you use, how long you spend on the site, or what QR codes you generate.
  • No Device Information: We do not collect information about your device, browser, operating system, or IP address.
  • No Location Data: We do not access or store your geographic location.
  • No Cookies: We do not use cookies, local storage, or any persistent tracking mechanisms.
  • No Analytics: We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any other analytics services.
  • No Third-Party Trackers: No advertising networks, data brokers, or marketing companies have access to your activity.

2.2 Information That Never Leaves Your Device

The following information is processed entirely within your web browser and is never transmitted to our servers or any third parties:

  • Wi-Fi network name (SSID)
  • Wi-Fi password
  • Security type selection (WPA/WPA2, WEP, or open network)
  • Hidden network preference
  • QR code size and error correction settings
  • Export format preference (PNG or SVG)

Technical Explanation: All processing happens client-side using JavaScript. The QR code is generated in your browser's memory using the QRCode.js library. Nothing is sent to a backend server because there is no backend server.

3. How We Use Information

Since we do not collect any information, we do not use any information. The data you enter into Wi-Fi QR Code Generator is used solely for the purpose of generating a QR code in your browser session. Once you close the tab or refresh the page, all entered information is permanently deleted.

The only "use" of your data is:

  1. Generating the Wi-Fi payload string in your browser's memory
  2. Creating the QR code visual representation using the QRCode.js library
  3. Displaying the QR code on your screen for scanning or download

That's it. No profiling, no marketing, no data mining, no selling to third parties.

4. Data Storage and Retention

4.1 No Server Storage

Wi-Fi QR Code Generator does not have a database or server-side storage. We cannot store your data even if we wanted to, because your data never reaches our servers.

4.2 Browser Session Only

Your Wi-Fi credentials exist only in your browser's temporary memory (RAM) during your active session. This data is:

  • Not saved to your device's storage
  • Not saved to browser localStorage or sessionStorage
  • Not included in browser history
  • Permanently deleted when you close the tab or refresh the page

4.3 Downloaded Files

If you choose to download a generated QR code, the file is saved to your device by your browser. This file contains only the visual QR code image - it does not contain your Wi-Fi password in plain text. The password is encoded within the QR code pattern itself, which can only be read by scanning the code with a QR reader.

5. Third-Party Services

5.1 CDN for Library Loading

Wi-Fi QR Code Generator uses the jsDelivr CDN (Content Delivery Network) to load the QRCode.js library. When you visit our site, your browser makes a request to jsDelivr's servers to download this library. This is a standard practice for web development.

What jsDelivr may see: Your IP address and browser user-agent string (standard for any web request). They do not receive your Wi-Fi credentials or any data you enter into the generator.

Offline Use: For complete offline use, you can download the QRCode.js library and host it locally. Instructions are available in our README documentation.

5.2 Google Fonts

Our website uses fonts from Google Fonts. When you visit our site, your browser loads these fonts from Google's servers. Google may log this request but does not receive any information you enter into the QR code generator.

5.3 GitHub Pages

Our website is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub's standard server logs may record basic access information (IP address, timestamp, requested page) for security and operational purposes. This is standard for any website and is governed by GitHub's own privacy policy.

5.4 No Third-Party Trackers

We do not use:

  • Google Analytics or similar analytics platforms
  • Facebook Pixel or social media tracking
  • Advertising networks or retargeting pixels
  • Heat mapping or session recording tools
  • Email marketing or newsletter tracking

6. Security

While we do not collect or store your data, we take security seriously in the design of our service:

  • HTTPS Encryption: Our website uses HTTPS to encrypt the connection between your browser and our server, protecting against man-in-the-middle attacks.
  • No Data Transmission: Since your Wi-Fi credentials never leave your device, there is no risk of interception during transmission.
  • Open Source: Our code is publicly available on GitHub, allowing security researchers and the community to review and audit it.
  • Minimal Dependencies: We use minimal third-party libraries, reducing the attack surface.
  • Regular Updates: We monitor our dependencies for security vulnerabilities and update them promptly.

7. Children's Privacy

Wi-Fi QR Code Generator is a general-purpose tool suitable for users of all ages. We do not knowingly collect any information from children under 13 (or the applicable age of digital consent in your jurisdiction). Since we do not collect any information from any users, this policy applies universally.

Parents and guardians can feel confident that children using this service are not being tracked, profiled, or exposed to data collection practices.

8. Your Rights

Under various privacy regulations (including GDPR, CCPA, and others), users have certain rights regarding their personal data. Since Wi-Fi QR Code Generator does not collect or store any personal data, these rights are inherently protected:

  • Right to Access: There is no data to access.
  • Right to Rectification: There is no data to correct.
  • Right to Erasure: There is no data to delete (closing your tab accomplishes this).
  • Right to Portability: There is no data to transfer.
  • Right to Object: There is no processing to object to.
  • Right to Opt-Out: You are automatically opted out of all tracking by design.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your privacy.
